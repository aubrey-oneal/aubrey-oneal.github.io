Carbon for AI Chat
Role: design lead
July 2025 - Dec 2025
By the time IBM turned its attention to creating a unified AI chat system, many product teams had independently created their own chat libraries, interaction models, and prompt experiences in response to rapid innovation in the company and external market pressure. However, no formal AI chat library existed, and teams increasingly needed an out of the box implementation with clear usage guidance. My job was to be the interface between product teams and the Carbon Design System team, surfacing existing solutions, identifying shared user needs, and consolidating fragmented efforts into a system grounded in practical implementation.
To drive alignment between designers and engineers distrubted across several teams, I established agile design ceremonies to review actual product use cases, compare approaches, and prioritize the features that mattered most. I translated market insights and user research into reusable UX patterns and extended Carbon components to accelerate production while maintaining accessibility and consistency.
Key contributions and impact:
  • Adopted by 20+ product teams across IBM for AI chat and agentic experiences.
  • Shipped in 10+ products, several featured at THINK 2025.
  • Developed reusable UX patterns and Carbon component extensions, reducing duplication and speeding production.
  • Designed the prompt bar experience, centralizing user intent, feedback, and control.
  • Built with extensibility in mind: Carbon AI Chat allows teams to implement unique workflowswhile relying on the stability of the Carbon library.
  • Collaborated with engineers to ensure library releases were achievable from an implementation standpoint.
The v1 release delivered a stable, accessible, and well-documented library, providing a shared foundation for differentiated AI experiences. Many additional features are on the roadmap, enabling teams to continue innovating on top of this system.
